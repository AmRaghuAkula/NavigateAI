The NavigateAI Framework

The 3D Scoring Framework

Score every problem statement from Stage 2 across three dimensions. Each dimension gets a score of 1–3. The total — out of 9 — tells you where to start. No gut feel. No politics. Just a transparent score anyone can interrogate.

D1
Business Value
How much does solving this actually matter?
Score the downstream business impact — on revenue, cost, customer experience, or risk — if this problem were solved completely. Be specific. Estimate in time saved, cost reduced, or revenue protected.
Low impact (1)
Meaningful impact (2)
High / compounding impact (3)
D2
Implementation Feasibility
How hard is this to actually build and deploy?
Score the technical and organisational complexity. Consider data availability, integration requirements, change management effort, and whether your team has the capability or a clear path to get it.
Complex / high effort (1)
Moderate effort (2)
Low effort / clear path (3)
D3
Data Readiness
Is the data actually there to support it?
Score the availability, quality, and accessibility of the data this problem depends on. A high-value problem with no usable data is not a quick win — it's a prerequisite project disguised as an AI initiative.
Data not accessible or clean (1)
Partial — needs preparation (2)
Clean, accessible, sufficient (3)
The scoring rule Score 7–9: pursue now. Score 4–6: pursue once a prerequisite is resolved. Score 1–3: park it — the foundations aren't there yet.

Where Your Problems Live

The Priority Zones

Once scored, every problem falls into one of four zones. Your sequence follows naturally — start in the top left, plan for the rest. Avoid the common mistake of starting in the top right because it's exciting.

✦ Start here
Quick Wins
High value, high feasibility, data ready. These are your first 90-day initiatives. They build organisational confidence, demonstrate ROI, and fund what comes next.
→ Commit, scope, and deploy within 90 days
Plan for wave 2
Strategic Bets
High value, lower feasibility or data gaps. These are worth investing in — but they need groundwork first. Plan them in parallel while quick wins are being delivered.
→ Identify the blocking prerequisite and assign an owner
Reassess
Easy but Low Return
Feasible but limited business impact. These feel tempting because they're achievable, but they consume capacity without moving meaningful metrics. Reassess or deprioritise.
→ Only pursue if they unlock something larger
Park it
Not Ready Yet
Low feasibility and limited near-term return. These aren't the right problems for now — either the foundations aren't there or the value doesn't justify the complexity at this stage.
→ Revisit in 6–12 months when conditions change
Horizontal axis: Business Value (low → high)
Vertical axis: Feasibility + Data Readiness (low → high)
Stage 3 — Take Action

Score your problems. Pick your first one.

Use the checklist to confirm your prioritisation is solid. Then use the scorer to work through your shortlist — one row per problem from Stage 2.

✦ Your shortlist is ready when…
Every problem from Stage 2 has been scored across all three dimensions
You have at least one problem scoring 7 or above — your first initiative
The first initiative has a named owner, a 90-day deadline, and defined success criteria
Problems scoring below 4 have been parked with a reason noted
Strategic Bets have identified blocking prerequisites with owners assigned
The shortlist has been shared with and agreed by your AI sponsor
✓ Common mistakes to avoid
Not starting with technically interesting problems over high-value ones
Not running more than two initiatives simultaneously in the first wave
Not letting a single advocate push a low-scored problem to the top of the list
