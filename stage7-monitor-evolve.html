The NavigateAI Framework

The AI Health Dashboard

Every live AI system should be tracked on five metrics. They take under 15 minutes to check weekly — and they catch 95% of the issues that cause live systems to quietly fail.

M1
Outcome
Is it still hitting the target?
Track the primary metric you defined in Stage 6 against your baseline. If the gap between target and actual is widening, something has changed — in the data, the tool, or the workflow.
e.g. "Report generated within 2 hours of Monday 9am — achieved 7/8 weeks. One failure due to API outage — documented."
M2
Accuracy
Are the outputs still correct?
Spot-check a sample of outputs against what a human would produce. If accuracy is declining, the model may be drifting or the underlying data may have changed in ways the system wasn't designed to handle.
e.g. "Spot-checked 10 outputs this week — 9/10 correct. One anomaly in the new product category data — flagged for investigation."
M3
Adoption
Are people actually using it?
A system that runs perfectly but isn't used has failed in the most important way. Track usage frequency and ask users directly whether the output is useful — not just whether the system is running.
e.g. "Report used by all 4 intended recipients every week. 3/4 say it saves significant time. 1 prefers manual version — investigating why."
M4
Cost
Is the cost still justified?
Track actual usage-based costs (API calls, compute, storage) against your model. AI tool costs can compound quickly at scale. Confirm the ROI case holds as usage grows.
e.g. "Monthly cost: $340 vs $320 estimated. Within tolerance. At projected growth rate, will hit $500 by Q3 — reassess pricing tier."
M5
Trust
Do users trust the output?
If users are double-checking every output or quietly reverting to manual processes, trust has eroded. This is the leading indicator of adoption failure — and it's invisible unless you ask.
e.g. "Monthly pulse: 'Do you trust this output without checking?' — 3/4 yes, 1 sometimes. No regression from last month."
The monitoring rule 15 minutes per week reviewing five metrics is all it takes to catch 95% of the issues that cause live AI systems to quietly fail. The cost of not monitoring is always higher.
Sustaining Performance Over Time

Three Review Cycles

Different questions require different cadences. These three review cycles ensure your AI system stays relevant, accurate, and aligned with your business as both evolve.

Weekly · 15 minutes
The Health Check
A quick scan of the five health metrics. Flag any anomaly. Log it. No action required unless something is clearly wrong.
Check outcome metric against baseline
Spot-check 3–5 recent outputs for accuracy
Confirm usage is on track
Review any system errors or failures from the week
Monthly · 1 hour
The Performance Review
A deeper look at trends across all five metrics. Is performance stable, improving, or declining? Does anything need to be escalated to the AI sponsor?
Review metric trends over the past 4 weeks
Collect user feedback — ask directly, not just via surveys
Review cost trajectory against the original ROI model
Identify any data quality changes that may be affecting outputs
Quarterly · Half-day
The Strategic Review
A broader assessment — is this system still solving the right problem? Has the business changed in ways that require the AI to be retrained, reconfigured, or replaced?
Review against the original problem statement from Stage 2
Assess whether the tool or model needs updating
Identify the next initiative to add to the pipeline from Stage 3
Update the AI Sponsor on the full portfolio status
Stage 7 — Take Action

Establish your monitoring. Build the habit.

Monitoring is a habit, not a system. Use the checklist to confirm your monitoring structure is in place, then use the worksheet to document your health dashboard for this deployment.

✦ Monitoring is active when…
All five health metrics have baselines recorded at launch
A named production owner has a weekly 15-minute monitoring slot blocked
A monthly performance review is scheduled with the AI Sponsor
A threshold for "pull from production" is documented — not just implied
Learnings from this deployment are captured for the next initiative
The next problem from Stage 3 has been identified and the cycle begins again
✓ Common mistakes to avoid
Not treating deployment as a milestone — monitoring starts on day one
Not waiting for something to break before establishing the review habit
Not letting the first deployment be the last — the value compounds with each one
