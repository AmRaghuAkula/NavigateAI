The NavigateAI Framework

The 3V Strategy Canvas

Three questions. Every AI strategy worth building can answer all three clearly. Complete this canvas before you look at a single tool or vendor.

V1
Vision
Where are you going?
Your AI vision is a business destination — not a technology statement. It describes what your organisation looks like when AI has done its job well.
What does winning look like in 3 years, and where does AI specifically accelerate that?
If AI freed up 20% of your team's time, what would you do with it?
Is your AI vision tied to your business strategy — or separate from it?
V2
Value
What will change?
Value is the specific, measurable difference AI will make. Not "improve efficiency" — but "reduce invoice processing from 4 hours to 20 minutes, weekly."
Which single business metric will AI most directly move?
What is the current baseline you're improving on?
How quickly should you realistically see that value?
V3
Velocity
How fast can you move?
Velocity is an honest audit of your organisation's capacity to act. Budget, decision authority, and change readiness all determine whether strategy stays on paper.
Who has the authority to approve and unblock AI initiatives?
What budget can you realistically commit in the next 12 months?
How does your team respond to meaningful process change?
The test "Can you describe your AI strategy in two sentences — with a specific outcome and a named owner?" If not, this canvas isn't complete yet.
Know Your Starting Point

The AI Journey Stages

You can't define where you're going until you're honest about where you are. Locate yourself before completing the 3V Canvas — your starting stage changes which actions matter most right now.

01
Observing
The Curious
AI is on your radar but no formal initiative exists. You're reading, watching, and wondering where to begin.
"We know we should probably do something, but nothing is committed yet."
02
Exploring
The Experimenter Most founders start here
Some informal tool usage or pilot attempts exist, but nothing has been measured against real business outcomes or reached production.
"We've tried a few things but nothing has officially landed yet."
03
Building
The Implementer
At least one AI initiative is live and delivering measurable value. You have a repeatable deployment process and growing team confidence.
"Our first agent is live. We're measuring results and planning the next use case."
04
Scaling
The Multiplier
AI is embedded in your operating model. You have governance, measurement, and a roadmap. The question is where to expand next, not whether to start.
"AI is a strategic asset. We're compounding gains and expanding systematically."
Stage 1 — Take Action

Complete before moving to Stage 2

Two things to do now: check that your foundation is solid, then fill in your 3V Canvas. Both take under 20 minutes and will sharpen every decision that follows.

✦ Your strategy is solid when…
You have a written AI vision tied to a specific business destination
AI goals are linked to at least one measurable business metric
Leadership shares a common understanding of AI's role
A named person owns the AI agenda with real authority
You know your current AI Journey Stage
You can describe your AI strategy in two clear sentences
✓ Traps to avoid
Not selecting tools before you've defined the problem
Not automating undocumented or broken processes
Not starting a pilot without pre-defined success criteria
